Dr. Ronald Loui

D Ronald P. Loui, Ph.D., was an active contributor to artificial intelligence for twenty years as a tenured professor at Washington University in St. Louis, 1987-2007. His undergraduate thesis won an ACM award (he wrote about information warfare as early as 1981, after helping found the Harvard International Review Military/Technology section). His doctoral thesis was funded by the U.S. Army Signals Warfare. In 1989 he consulted on sensor data fusion for the F-18 with McDonnell-Douglas. In 2001, he and John Lockwood (AlgoLogic/Stanford) initiated a project to do deep packet inspection, packet rewriting, and semantic document stream classification in network hardware. From 2002-2007, the DoD began funding a top secret project with this team through SAIC. Loui worked on foreign language problems for the NSA with A. Ratner. Loui left academia to work on HIPAA-secured electronic medical records and semantic database projects at Cycorp. He returned to academia two and half years ago to teach cyberwarfare at UIS. Loui recently sat on two DHS funding panels
