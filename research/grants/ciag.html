Equipment Donation Grant from the Critical Infrastructure Assurance Group (CIAG)

 

Dr. Ted Mims , Principle Investigator, Total amount of $145,890.

Abstract: The Critical Infrastructure Assurance Group provides leadership to improve the security of global critical infrastructure. CIAG is committed to helping organizations worldwide implement security strategies by directly addressing research, training, education, sound practices, and standards needs.  This grant was based on the success of the CSSIA grant and the positive relationship we have developed with the CIAG.  Robert J. Du Charme, CCSP, ISSP, Security Training Manager and Danny Rodriguez, CISSP, CCSP Security Education Engineer for the CIAG have provided consultation and training for our faculty.  In the summer 2006 Danny Rodriguez taught a Cisco boot camp that Dr. Allan Roth, instructor for the intrusion detection course, attended.  In the summer 2007 Robert DeCharme taught a Cisco boot camp that was hosted by CSSIA and Illinois Working Connections Institute.  Janis Rose, instructor for the firewall implementation course, attended the boot camp as did several community college faculty members that also attended the Working Connections Institute.  Mr. Rodriguez and Mr. DuCharme provide excellent training to the teachers and discussed future trends in information assurance as well as how we should adjust the curriculum to prepare students for the work place.  At UIS, we have taken their advice and used the $145,890 donated equipment to establish intrusion detection and firewall implementation laboratories to enhance an existing Cisco Systems Security and Information Assurance laboratory that was previously established through the National Science Foundation/Advanced Technological Education CSSIA Grant.
